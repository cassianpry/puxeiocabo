# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Brazilian Street Fighter 6 competitive players — from casual ranked grinders to tournament-level competitors. They play on PC, PS5, or both. Their context: they just finished a ranked match where the opponent disconnected to avoid a loss, and they want to report it quickly while the memory is fresh. They're frustrated but looking for a constructive outlet — community accountability, not just venting.

## Product Purpose

Puxei o Cabo is the community's rage-quit reporting system for Street Fighter 6. Players submit match screenshots as proof of disconnects, and administrators review them using EXIF metadata analysis to detect fake or AI-generated images. The goal: make rage-quitting visible, track repeat offenders, and give the Brazilian SF6 community a tool to self-police the competitive environment.

## Brand Personality

**Playful, sharp, clear.** The voice is confident but never aggressive — like a tournament referee who's seen it all. Portuguese (pt-BR) throughout, with the warmth and directness of the Brazilian fighting game community. The tone is serious when it needs to be (reporting, moderation) but relaxed in navigation and helper text. Never corporate, never juvenile.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS-corporate** (Stripe, Linear, Vercel marketing) — this is not a business tool
- **Neon/cyberpunk/RGB-gamer aesthetic** — no excessive glow, no forced "gaming" tropes
- **Social media clones** — not Twitter, not Reddit, not a forum
- **Generic dark SaaS** — not just another dark-themed admin panel with nothing to say
- **False authority** — no badges, trust seals, or "enterprise" signaling

## Design Principles

1. **Honest by default** — the app exists to call out dishonesty. The interface itself must feel trustworthy, transparent, never deceptive. Clear status indicators, straightforward error messages, no dark patterns.

2. **Visual evidence first** — the core action is submitting and reviewing proof images. Layouts should center the image, not bury it under chrome. Thumbnails, lightboxes, and image-first cards take priority over dense tables.

3. **Playful precision** — gaming community energy meets a serious function. Copy should have personality without sacrificing clarity. Interactions should feel responsive and satisfying without being flashy.

4. **Community-owned** — built for the Brazilian SF6 community. Language, tone, and design conventions should reflect that. Every screen should feel like it belongs to the players, not to a faceless platform.

5. **Respect the user's time** — reporting a rage-quitter is a quick task. Fast interactions, clear status, minimal friction. No unnecessary steps, no modal-first thinking.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard web best practices. No legal WCAG requirements. The app is already pt-BR only, and the community is gaming-focused, so screen reader support and keyboard navigation should follow shadcn/ui's built-in defaults. Reduced motion preferences should be respected for any animations.
